# FTP vs PT-T: Positioning Reference

**Status:** CANONICAL
**Date:** 2026-07-12
**Purpose:** Reference definitions and relationship between FTP and PT-T for all marketing, editorial, and product copy.

---

## FTP (Strict Definition)

Functional Threshold Power. Coined by Andrew Coggan. The highest power a rider can maintain in a quasi-steady state without fatiguing, for approximately one hour. A physiological measurement: a proxy for maximal lactate steady state (MLSS). Expressed as a single number in watts. Estimated in practice via 20-minute test x 0.95, 8-minute protocols, or ramp tests. Static until retested.

One question answered: how hard.

## The Sport's FTP (Pragmatic Reduction)

Athletes and coaches adopted FTP as an identity and progress framework. This applied version carries intuitions about duration, progression, and feel that the strict definition never intended to support. Athletes asking "is my FTP going up?" or "I feel like I can hold my threshold longer" are reaching for something FTP does not have the dimensions to answer.

FTP was designed as a measurement. The sport uses it as a mirror. Those are two different jobs.

## PT-T (Definition)

The seventh seat in the Power Threshold Array. A coordinate in power x offset space representing where the threshold energy system transition occurs, detected from the density of accumulated qualifying work across durations, weighted toward the recent.

Two dimensions: power (watts) and offset (seconds). Continuous detection from racing. No prescribed test. Updated as new data enters the pool.

## The Relationship

PT-T formalizes the sport's applied FTP. It gives the informal concept two named dimensions (power and offset), continuous detection from racing (no test), and a framework for the questions athletes were already asking but had no language for: how long, in what direction, and how is it changing.

The strict FTP is untouched. PT-T is not arguing with Coggan. It picks up where the sport's informal application of his concept ran out of resolution.

## Positioning Rules

- PT-T is not a replacement for FTP. Never claim this.
- PT-T is not a better FTP. It detects a different thing that lives near the same physiological boundary.
- PT-T formalizes what athletes already feel but could not name.
- The shared ground: both live near the lactate steady-state boundary. The difference: FTP measures capacity at that boundary (one dimension, one number, one test). PT-T detects the transition coordinate (two dimensions, continuous, from racing).
- The unlock question: "What's your FTP in minutes?"

## Algorithm Requirement (Derived)

The algorithm must detect the threshold transition coordinate accurately enough that an athlete recognizes it as the thing they already feel, updated continuously from their own riding, without ever requiring them to perform a test. If the number disagrees with their felt experience and the disagreement cannot be explained, the algorithm has failed.
